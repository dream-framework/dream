Our 4D world is a finite-resolution projection of a compact, atemporal 10D Meta-Manifold (MM). A single fixed kernel pushes 10D fields into 4D observables; fine detail is averaged out, projection invariants survive. Kernel parameters act as axioms of our slice.

DREAM in plain words

Think of reality as rich high-dimensional structure seen through one fixed “lens.” Because the lens has limited resolution, only sturdy patterns get through into our 4D world. Those sturdy patterns—organization, conserved categories, stable spectra—are what experiments can actually check.

Read more

Instead of guessing the exact lens, we focus on what about the lens really matters for data: a critical resolution scale (how fine we can see), how local the mapping is (nearby things stay related), a regularity condition (no pathological behavior), and an “effective spectral exponent” that summarizes how many patterns survive as we zoom.

What this means for physics

  • Why constants feel fundamental: they behave like stable “lens settings” for our slice of reality.
  • Two regimes: below the lens scale we keep delicate structure (quantum-grade); above it we only see coarse patterns (classical).
  • How we test it: make forecasts that depend only on those stable settings, not on hidden details of the lens.

What could show up soon

S1 — Non-harmonic “rungs”

A set of axion-like frequencies with a specific first “rung” and then rungs that are not evenly spaced. Hitting the first rung and the spacing pattern would be a strong signature.

S2 — Coherence “cliff”

When an experiment sweeps across the lens scale, interference/contrast should change abruptly—like stepping off a curb rather than walking down a ramp.

S3 — Extra polarization

Space-based interferometers could see tiny strain patterns in the mHz band linked to very energetic sources—an extra polarization component beyond the usual.

How we’d know we’re wrong

  • No first rung and no matching spacing pattern, despite sufficient sensitivity.
  • No abrupt change in interference near the expected scale, across multiple kinds of experiments.
  • No extra-polarization patterns at levels our models say would be visible.

Why this is still science

The forecasts don’t rely on hidden details. They only use the stable parts of the lens that matter for data. That’s why a clear negative result really counts.

Abstract — plain

DREAM treats 4D physics as a measurement of invariants of a 10→4 projection. Because the kernel has finite resolving power, much 10D structure is packed into each 4D locus. What survives are projection invariants (large-scale organization, conserved classes, robust spectra); sub-resolution detail averages out. Conventional “constants” are treated as kernel-fixed parameters of our slice.

The program emphasizes testable statements that do not depend on picking a detailed kernel inside a well-posed class. Instead we assert only operational invariants of the kernel: resolution scale \(\lambda_q\), locality radius (peakedness near \(x\!\approx\!\pi(X)\)), regularity/positivity (PSD behavior), and the effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\) that controls mode counting, masses and entropy.

Abstract — precise

4D observables are modeled as push-forwards of MM fields by a fixed kernel \(K_\lambda\) (finite resolution \(\lambda\)):

\[ O(x)=\!\!\int_{\mathrm{MM}} K_\lambda(x;X)\,\Phi(X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X),\qquad \lambda=\lambda_q. \]

Only invariants of \(K\) matter empirically; the detailed choice inside a well-posed class is not identified by data.

  • K1 (normalization): projected norms are finite.
  • K2 (locality-in-projection): \(K_\lambda\) sharply peaks for \(x\approx\pi(X)\).
  • K3 (monotone fidelity): retention \(\alpha(\lambda)\) decreases with \(\lambda\).
\[ \alpha(\lambda)=\exp\!\Big[-\big(\tfrac{\lambda}{\lambda_q}\big)^{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\Big]. \]

Key Axioms (capsule)

  • A1 — Existence (MM). A compact, orientable, smooth 10D manifold with fields; atemporal in 4D terms.
  • A2 — Projection (kernel). A fixed, non-invertible finite-resolution kernel; only its invariants/parameters are empirical.
  • A3 — All-to-four. Mapping is 10→4; no global split \(U_4\times K_6\) required.
  • A4 — Constants are projection-fixed. \(\{c,\hbar,G,\ldots\}\) are kernel parameters in our slice.
  • A5 — Topology ↔ classes. Stable MM topological data label particle/gauge classes.
  • A6 — Finite resolution ⇒ regimes. Below \(\lambda_q\): quantum-grade invariants; above: classical coarse-grain.
