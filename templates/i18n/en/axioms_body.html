Core Axioms

Six assumptions define the posture; everything else is derived or tested at the level of invariants.

This page states the axioms plainly, gives their operational meaning, and shows where the math enters. Math is hidden by default—use the “Math” toggle in the header.

Capsule — the six axioms

  1. A1 — Existence (MM). A compact, orientable, smooth 10‑dimensional Meta‑Manifold with fields exists; it is atemporal in 4D terms.
  2. A2 — Projection (Kernel). A single, fixed, non‑invertible kernel realizes 10→4 projection at finite resolution.
  3. A3 — All‑to‑four. No global split \(U_4\times K_6\) is required; all 10 coordinates contribute to 4D observables.
  4. A4 — Constants are projection‑fixed. \(\{c,\,\hbar,\,G,\,\ldots\}\) are parameters of our slice of the kernel, not derived numbers.
  5. A5 — Topology ↔ classes. Stable MM topological data label particle/gauge classes that project to 4D content.
  6. A6 — Finite resolution ⇒ regimes. Below a critical scale \(\lambda_q\) “quantum‑grade” invariants retain; above it classical coarse‑grain dominates.

Axioms deliberately separate what is assumed (kernel parameters/invariants exist) from what is tested (relationships among invariants and forecasts).

A1 — Existence (MM)

There is a compact, orientable, smooth 10D manifold (MM) with fields. “Atemporal” means 4D time is not a fundamental MM coordinate; it appears only after projection.

Why compact & smooth? Compactness ensures finite integrals; smoothness underwrites the differential operators used in push‑forwards and topological indices.

Empirical footprint. A1 itself is not probed directly; it supplies the stage on which the other axioms make testable contact with data.

A2 — Projection (Kernel)

A single, fixed, non‑invertible kernel \(K_{\lambda}\) maps MM fields to 4D observables at finite resolution \(\lambda\) (our slice takes \(\lambda=\lambda_q\)).

\[ O(x)=\int_{\mathrm{MM}} K_{\lambda}(x;X)\,\Phi(X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X). \]

Science attaches to invariants of the kernel class (resolution \(\lambda_q\), locality radius, regularity/positivity, effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\)).

Guardrails. Normalization, locality in projection, and monotone fidelity ensure a well‑posed map \(L^2(\mathrm{MM})\to L^2(\mathbb{R}^{3,1})\).

\[\textbf{K1:}\ \int K_{\lambda}(x;X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X)<\infty,\qquad \textbf{K2:}\ K_{\lambda}(x;X)\text{ peaks for }X\approx\pi^{-1}(x),\qquad \textbf{K3:}\ \partial_{\lambda}\alpha(\lambda)\le0.\]

A3 — All‑to‑four

No required factorization \(U_4\times K_6\). All ten coordinates can affect 4D observables through the push‑forward; this avoids model‑dependent internal splits.

Operationally. Forecasts are phrased in terms of invariants only, so they remain agnostic to any particular internal factor choice.

A4 — Constants are projection‑fixed

The constants \(\{c,\,\hbar,\,G,\,\ldots\}\) are parameters of our kernel slice. We measure them; we do not derive them inside D.R.E.A.M.

Implication. Varying‑constant scenarios would correspond to different projection conditions, not to dynamical evolution within a fixed kernel class.

A5 — Topology ↔ classes

Stable MM topological data (homotopy/cohomology, characteristic classes) label particle/gauge classes. Individual 4D instances are projections of those classes.

Quantization. Integer‑valued integrals over cycles provide charge quantization that is robust to finite‑resolution averaging.

\[Q_{\mathrm{U(1)}}=\frac{1}{2\pi}\oint A\in\mathbb{Z},\qquad k=\frac{1}{8\pi^2}\int\operatorname{tr}(F\wedge F)\in\mathbb{Z}.\]

Selection rules. Class addition/subtraction constrains allowed processes; anomalies must cancel in the projected 4D content.

A6 — Finite resolution ⇒ regimes

A fidelity law separates two regimes with a coherence “cliff” near \(\lambda\approx\lambda_q\):

\[ \alpha(\lambda)=\exp\!\Big[-\big(\tfrac{\lambda}{\lambda_q}\big)^{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\Big]. \]

Below \(\lambda_q\) (“quantum‑grade”) fine structure retains; above it classical coarse‑grain dominates.

Experimental handle. Scan across \(\lambda_q\) in controlled platforms (interferometry, photonics, NV, etc.) and look for the step‑like change in coherence.

Assumed vs. tested

Assumed

  • MM exists and is smooth & compact (A1).
  • Single fixed kernel at finite resolution (A2).
  • All‑to‑four posture (A3).
  • Constants are kernel‑fixed (A4).

Tested (invariants)

  • Retention law near \(\lambda_q\) (A6): detect the transition.
  • Topological quantization/selection rules in 4D (A5).
  • Non‑harmonic ladder controlled by \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\) (ALP base & ratios).

Falsification hooks tied to axioms

  • Against A6. No retention transition near the posited \(\lambda_q\) across platforms at forecasted sensitivity.
  • Against A5. Violation of integer quantization or selection rules inconsistent with the class picture.
  • Against the invariant posture. Exclusion of the ALP base window and non‑harmonic ratios \(m_n/m_1=n^{1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\) at forecasted sensitivity.

These are clean nulls: we do not rescue the framework by unconstrained kernel tweaks.

Interpretive note (speculative)

Constants feel “fundamental” only because they are kernel‑fixed parameters in our slice; alternative local projection conditions might shift some of them. This is interpretive and not required for empirical tests.
