Falsification

Clean fail gates phrased only in terms of kernel invariants and instrument‑level observables.

This page defines what would decisively falsify each near‑term claim, how to pre‑register analyses, and how to interpret nulls. Math is hidden by default — toggle “Math” in the header.

What “falsification” means in DREAM

  • Invariant‑level claims. Tests are stated in terms of kernel invariants (resolution \(\lambda_q\), effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\)) and directly measured quantities.
  • No unconstrained rescues. We do not “save” a failed claim by changing the kernel form; only pre‑declared invariant ranges may be updated with new data.
  • Multi‑platform bias check. Where feasible, confirm with orthogonal platforms (photonic, atom, NV, etc.) to rule out instrument‑specific artifacts.

Pre‑registration protocol

  1. Declare invariants: prior ranges for \(\lambda_q\) and \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\), with rationale.
  2. Map instrument scale: how the platform’s baseline/wavelength/coherence maps to the effective \(\lambda\).
  3. Fix windows & ladders: base window and next rungs using \(m_n/m_1=n^{1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\).
  4. State fail gates ahead of time: thresholds for exclusion, visibility changes, and template upper bounds.
  5. Publish analysis: pipelines, injection campaigns, and data snippets sufficient for re‑analysis.

Fail gate S1 — ALP non‑harmonic ladder

Claim: a light axion‑like tower with base \(m_1\approx 65\,\mathrm{neV}\) (\(f_1\approx 15.717\,\mathrm{MHz}\)) and ratios

\[ \tfrac{m_n}{m_1}=n^{1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}}. \]

Pre‑registered window. List the scanned span around \(f_1\) and planned rungs for assumed \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\).

Exposure reporting. Provide per‑mode exposure (couplings, quality factors, backgrounds).

Decisive null: the base window and the non‑harmonic ratios are excluded at the forecasted sensitivity (per mode) → the ladder claim fails.

Negative at the base alone is suggestive but not decisive unless the ratio pattern is also excluded for plausible \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\).

Fail gate S2 — Coherence cliff near \(\lambda_q\)

Claim: visibility/contrast follows the retention law and exhibits a transition as the effective probe crosses \(\lambda_q\):

\[ \alpha(\lambda)=\exp\!\Big[-\big(\tfrac{\lambda}{\lambda_q}\big)^{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\Big]. \]

Scan design. Sweep \(\lambda\) across the posited \(\lambda_q\) in at least two independent platforms; record visibility, phase noise, and control channels.

Model comparison. Compare fits to the retention law vs. smooth‑no‑cliff baselines using pre‑declared criteria (e.g., Bayes factors, information metrics).

Decisive null: repeated scans across the posited \(\lambda_q\) show no transition within uncertainties across platforms → the retention law fails as stated.

Fail gate S3 — Torsion / extra polarization

Claim: template families with strain amplitude

\[ h_T\sim\epsilon_T\,\frac{G\,E}{c^4 r} \]

describe a detectable channel in the mHz band for large‑energy hubs.

Template set. Include polarization content, source energetics \(E\), and distances \(r\); pre‑register on/off‑source windows and vetoes.

Calibration. Use injection campaigns and end‑to‑end recovery efficiency to set bounds.

Decisive null: null at modeled levels across templates/epochs → this channel fails.

Decision tree (reading outcomes)

  • S1 null + S2 null + S3 null → invariant posture fails globally (ladder + retention + torsion all unsupported).
  • S1 null + S2 hit → ladder claim fails; retention law survives. Re‑estimate \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\) from cliff; update ladder windows if warranted.
  • S1 hit + S2 null → tension; revisit instrument mapping of \(\lambda\) and nuisance models. If robust, the retention law is in doubt.
  • S1 hit + S2 hit → strongest support; use joint fit to tighten \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\).

Reporting checklist

  • State invariant priors and instrument mapping (\(\lambda\) vs. knobs).
  • Provide windows, per‑mode exposure, and explicit fail gates.
  • Release analysis code and injection studies; include null channels and sanity checks.
  • Document systematics and how they are treated (as nuisance, not absorbed into invariants).

Robustness & edge cases

  • Partial coverage: if some rungs are unscanned, claims remain conditional; do not declare global null.
  • Bandwidth/line shape: broadened or split lines should be modeled as nuisance; the ratio law remains the discriminator.
  • Platform‑specific artifacts: use orthogonal platforms and blinded analyses to rule them out.

Interpretive note (speculative)

Even nulls can be informative about organizational limits of the projection kernel — e.g., upper bounds on \(\lambda_q\) or constraints on effective exponents that shape density‑of‑states. This framing is optional and not required for the scientific claims.
