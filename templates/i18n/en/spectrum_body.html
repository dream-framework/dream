Intuition

D.R.E.A.M compresses a 10D Meta‑Manifold (MM) into 4D with a finite‑resolution kernel. The compression leaves a spectral footprint summarized by an effective exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\): it governs how many modes survive up to a scale and how nearby masses/frequencies are spaced.

Instead of postulating a literal fractal manifold, we package the observable consequences in \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\), which enters lab spectra, entropy scaling, and cosmological cross‑checks.

Effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\)

The cumulative mode count below a cutoff \(\Omega\) scales as

\[ N(\Omega)\;\propto\;\Omega^{\,D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\quad\Rightarrow\quad\rho(\Omega)=\tfrac{dN}{d\Omega}\propto\Omega^{\,D_{\mathrm{eff}}-1}. \]

Here \(\rho\) is the density of states. The same exponent appears in band‑limited projections produced by the finite‑resolution kernel.

Non‑harmonic mass/frequency ladder

A light axion‑like tower exhibits non‑harmonic spacing governed by \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\):

\[ \frac{m_n}{m_1}=n^{\,1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}},\qquad m_1\approx 65\,\mathrm{neV}\ \Rightarrow\ f_1\approx 15.717\,\mathrm{MHz}. \]

These numbers fix concrete windows for haloscopes and distinguish D.R.E.A.M’s ladder from linear KK spectra.

Spacing behavior. For \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}>1\) the ratio \(m_{n+1}/m_n\) decreases with \(n\), producing a compressed ladder; for \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}<1\) it spreads faster than harmonic.

Couplings. Mode‑dependent couplings can vary across the tower; forecasts should report exposure assumptions per mode rather than a single universal coupling.

Instrument mapping

  • Haloscopes: center searches around \(m_1\sim 65\,\mathrm{neV}\) (\(f_1\approx15.717\,\mathrm{MHz}\)); check ratios \(n^{1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\) for the next rungs.
  • Interferometry/photonic platforms: use the retention cliff near \(\lambda_q\) to bracket \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\) jointly with spectral fits.
  • Space‑based interferometers: cross‑link torsion/extra‑polarization templates with the same exponent where relevant.

Report both base exposure (for the first rung) and per‑mode assumptions; D.R.E.A.M’s key discriminator is the ratio law, not just the base hit.

Density of states & entropy

Asymptotically, the number of band‑limited degrees of freedom in a region of size \(R\) scales like

\[ \mathcal{N}(R)\;\sim\;\big(\tfrac{R}{\lambda_q}\big)^{\!D_{\mathrm{eff}}}, \]

suggesting an effective entropy scaling at fixed bandwidth. This provides cosmological and structural cross‑checks against surveys and background spectra.

Cosmology & structure cross‑checks

  • Compare exponents inferred from large‑scale structure / background spectra to lab‑inferred \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\).
  • Entropy and correlation‑length trends should be consistent with the \(\mathcal{N}(R)\) scaling.
  • Divergences between cosmology and lab fits provide leverage for falsification.

Worked capsule (didactic)

Assume cumulative count \(N(\Omega)\propto \Omega^{\,D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\). If boundary conditions select discrete modes with monotone indices \(n\), then \(n\sim N(m_n)\Rightarrow m_n\propto n^{1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\), yielding the ladder law above.
