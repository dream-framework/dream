Mathematical Frame

Minimal but complete formalization used throughout DREAM.

This page gathers the definitions, assumptions, and working formulas. Math is grouped into small capsules—use the expander arrows to dive deeper. All equations respect the Math toggle.

Notation & objects

  • Meta‑Manifold (MM): a compact, orientable, smooth 10‑manifold with metric \(g_{\mathrm{MM}}\) and volume form \(d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}\).
  • Projection map: \(\pi:\;\mathrm{MM}\to \mathbb{R}^{3,1}\) (topological surjection).
  • Kernel: \(K_{\lambda}(x;X)\ge 0\) with resolution parameter \(\lambda\) (our slice takes \(\lambda=\lambda_q\)).
  • Fields: MM fields \(\Phi\) (bundles suppressed); 4D observables \(O\).
  • Invariants: \(\lambda_q\) (resolution), locality radius, regularity/positivity, effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\).

Push‑forward definition

4D observables are push‑forwards of MM fields via the kernel:

\[ O(x)=\int_{\mathrm{MM}} K_{\lambda}(x;X)\,\Phi(X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X),\qquad \lambda=\lambda_q. \]

Only invariants of the kernel class are empirical; the detailed functional form of \(K\) is not identified by data.

Linearity and boundedness. For fixed \(x\), the map \(\Phi\mapsto O(x)\) is linear. We assume the operator \(\mathcal{K}_{\lambda}:\, L^2(\mathrm{MM})\to L^2(\mathbb{R}^{3,1})\) induced by \(K_{\lambda}\) is bounded.

Normalization. It is convenient to choose \(\int K_{\lambda}(x;X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X)=1\) so that constants push‑forward to constants.

Measure compatibility. All statements are invariant under equivalent choices of volume forms, provided \(K_{\lambda}d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}\) is used consistently.

Kernel guardrails (well‑posedness)

\[\textbf{K1 (normalization)}:\quad \int K_{\lambda}(x;X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X) < \infty.\]
\[\textbf{K2 (locality in projection)}:\quad K_{\lambda}(x;X)\text{ sharply peaks for }X\approx \pi^{-1}(x).\]
\[\textbf{K3 (monotone fidelity)}:\quad \alpha(\lambda)\text{ decreases with }\lambda,\ \alpha(\lambda_q)\approx 1.\]

Regularity/positivity. We work with kernels that are positive semidefinite as operators, ensuring non‑negative quadratic forms and stable numerics.

Examples. Gaussian‑type kernels on normal coordinates are a didactic proxy. DREAM remains kernel‑agnostic; only invariants are used in forecasts.

Fidelity & retention law

Fidelity \(\alpha(\lambda)\in[0,1]\) quantifies how much fine structure retains when viewed at effective scale \(\lambda\). A convenient invariant parametrization is

\[ \alpha(\lambda)=\exp\!\Big[-\big(\tfrac{\lambda}{\lambda_q}\big)^{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\Big]. \]

Two regimes follow naturally: for \(\lambda\ll\lambda_q\) high‑fidelity (“quantum‑grade”) invariants retain; for \(\lambda\gg\lambda_q\) coarse‑grain dominates, producing a coherence cliff near \(\lambda\approx\lambda_q\).

Monotonicity. By construction the derivative \(\partial_{\lambda}\alpha(\lambda)\le 0\). Empirically, scans across \(\lambda_q\) should register a step‑like change in interference/coherence observables.

Instrument mapping. The effective scale \(\lambda\) is platform‑dependent (baseline, wavelength, coherence length); only ratios to \(\lambda_q\) matter.

Effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\)

Instead of literal fractal manifolds, DREAM packages spectral counting into an effective exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\). This controls mode growth and enters lab observables.

Mass/frequency ladder. For a light axion‑like tower one expects non‑harmonic spacing

\[\frac{m_n}{m_1}=n^{1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}},\qquad m_1\approx 65\,\mathrm{neV}\Rightarrow f_1\approx 15.717\,\mathrm{MHz}.\]

Entropy & density of states. The same exponent controls asymptotic counting in band‑limited projections and informs cosmological scaling cross‑checks.

Variation. Stationarity on MM followed by push‑forward yields Euler–Lagrange‑type relations for \(O\). Kernel invariants appear as parameters, not as dynamical fields.

The purpose of this section is bookkeeping, not micro‑modeling: forecasts are stated in invariant language so they do not depend on a specific kernel choice.

Consistency checklist

  • Locality in projection: \(K_{\lambda}(x;X)\) concentrated near \(X\approx\pi^{-1}(x)\).
  • Integrability/PSD: bounded operator with non‑negative quadratic form.
  • Normalization: constants map to constants.
  • Monotone fidelity: \(\alpha(\lambda)\) non‑increasing; critical scale \(\lambda_q\).
  • Gauge/topology interface: stable topological data on MM label 4D classes under projection.

Worked micro‑example (didactic)

Take a scalar \(\Phi\) on MM and a Gaussian proxy kernel with locality radius \(\sigma\):

\[ K_{\lambda}(x;X)\propto\exp\!\Big(-\tfrac{\mathrm{dist}(X,\pi^{-1}(x))^2}{2\sigma^2}\Big),\quad \sigma\sim\lambda. \]

Then \(O=K_{\lambda}\star\Phi\) is a band‑limited observable whose high‑frequency content is exponentially suppressed; the suppression index is controlled by \(\lambda/\lambda_q\), which feeds directly into the fidelity law.

Falsification gates (math form)

  • Retention: repeated null of a transition in \(\partial_{\lambda}\alpha\) near the posited \(\lambda_q\) across platforms.
  • Ladder: exclusion of the base mass window and the non‑harmonic ratios \(m_n/m_1=n^{1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\) at forecasted sensitivity.
  • Tensor/torsion: null results for strains consistent with \(h_T\sim\epsilon_T\,GE/(c^4 r)\) at the modeled levels.

The invariants \(\lambda_q\) and \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\) are parameters of the projection, not tunable fudge factors.

Notes

“Fractal” is used as an effective spectral exponent for mode counting (a well‑posed surrogate), not as a literal non‑integer manifold dimension.

Wherever possible we phrase claims as relationships among invariants; the detailed kernel choice is an implementation detail.
