Foundations

Is 4D fundamental?
No. In DREAM, 4D observables are push‑forwards of 10D fields on a compact Meta‑Manifold (MM) via a fixed, non‑invertible kernel at finite resolution. “Fundamental” structure lives on MM; 4D is a projection.
What is the projection kernel?
A linear map \(K_{\lambda}(x;X)\) tying MM points \(X\) to 4D points \(x\) at resolution \(\lambda\) (our slice uses \(\lambda_q\)). Observables:
\[O(x)=\int_{\mathrm{MM}} K_{\lambda}(x;X)\,\Phi(X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X).\]
Why not measure the kernel directly?
Multiple kernels with the same invariants yield indistinguishable observables at our resolution. Data constrain the invariants: resolution \(\lambda_q\), locality radius, regularity/positivity, effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\).
Is MM inside our spacetime?
No embedding is assumed. There is a surjection \(\pi:\mathrm{MM}\to\mathbb{R}^{3,1}\); 4D points are images of whole high‑D fibers \(\pi^{-1}(x)\). Metrics and lightcones live in 4D after projection.
What does “atemporal MM” mean?
4D time is not a fundamental MM coordinate. The only \(t\)-dependence enters via the 4D argument of the push‑forward; introducing an MM‑time adds an unidentifiable clock.
Do we require a split \(U_4\times K_6\)?
No. Axiom A3 (“all‑to‑four”) avoids a global factorization; all ten coordinates compress directly into 4D observables.

Kernel & invariants

Which quantities are invariant/empirical?
Four: (i) resolution \(\lambda_q\); (ii) locality radius; (iii) regularity/positivity (PSD operator); (iv) effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\).
How does fidelity depend on scale?

\[\alpha(\lambda)=\exp\!\Big[-\big(\tfrac{\lambda}{\lambda_q}\big)^{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\Big]\]

What is \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\)?
An effective spectral exponent packaging mode counting on the internal sector. It governs non‑harmonic spacing in the axion‑like ladder and enters entropy/cosmology scalings.
Are “fractal” claims literal?
No. “Fractal” is used as an effective spectral exponent; the working math stays on smooth surrogates.
Well‑posedness guardrails?
Normalization, locality in projection, and monotone fidelity define a bounded PSD operator \(L^2(\mathrm{MM})\to L^2(\mathbb{R}^{3,1})\).
