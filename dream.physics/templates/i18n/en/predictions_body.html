S1 — Low-energy Axion-Like Particles (ALPs)

Prediction: Existence of an ALP with mass 65 neV and a non-harmonic spectrum

\[ m_n = 65 \text{ neV} \times n^{1/1.72} \]

Experimental program:

Experiment Timeline Status

S2 — Quantum–classical transition at 10⁻¹² m

Prediction: A sharp drop in coherence at the scale λq ≈ 10⁻¹² m

Experimental program:

Experiment Institution Timeline
NV-center interferometry Delft University of Technology 2024–2025
Silicon qubit arrays MIT/Harvard 2025
Atomic interferometers Stanford 2024–2026

Method: Scan the interferometer baseline from 10⁻¹³ m to 10⁻¹¹ m while measuring fringe visibility.

Falsification criterion: Absence of a transition at λ = (1.0 ± 0.2) × 10⁻¹² m on three independent platforms by 2026.

S3 — Fractal structure of the Universe

Prediction: Change in fractal dimension ΔDh = 0.25 ± 0.05 at z = 1.2

✔ Recent observations: Dh ≈ 1.7 reported for the Saraswati supercluster

Analysis of the Saraswati supercluster (Raj et al., 2019; reaffirmed in subsequent 2025 reviews) indicates a fractal dimension in the range 1.75–2. This aligns with the DREAM prediction of a fractal cosmic web and supports the hypothesis ΔDh ~ 0.25 on intermediate scales.

Experimental program:

Telescope Data Analysis window
DESI (Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument) Five-year data analysis 2025+
Euclid (ESA) Cosmology results 2026
LSST (Vera C. Rubin Observatory) First light 2026

Method: Wavelet analysis of the galaxy/void distribution for 0.5 < z < 2.0.

Falsification criterion: |ΔDh| < 0.1 across z < 2.0 in DESI and Euclid data by 2025.
