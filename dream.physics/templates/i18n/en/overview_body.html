Big picture

In D.R.E.A.M, 4D observables are not bare pieces of the 10D reality (MM). They are push‑forwards through a single, fixed, non‑invertible projection kernel at finite resolution. Because the kernel has finite resolving power, fine details are averaged out. What survives in 4D are projection invariants—large‑scale organization, conserved classes, and robust spectra. Conventional “constants of nature” are treated as kernel‑fixed parameters of our slice.

Six axioms anchor the posture: (A1) the MM exists and is smooth and compact; (A2) a fixed kernel maps 10→4 at finite resolution; (A3) all‑to‑four—a global U4×K6 split is not required; (A4) constants like c, ℏ, G are projection‑fixed; (A5) stable MM topology labels particle/gauge classes; (A6) finite resolution implies two operational regimes (below the kernel scale “quantum‑grade” invariants retain; above it we recover classical coarse‑grain).

Kernel push‑forward (math gist)

The 4D field \(O\) is a push‑forward of MM fields \(\Phi\) by a kernel \(K_{\lambda}\) at resolution \(\lambda\):

\[ O(x)=\int_{\mathrm{MM}} K_{\lambda}(x;X)\,\Phi(X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X),\qquad \lambda=\lambda_q\ \text{(our slice)}. \]

Only invariants of the kernel class are empirical; the detailed form of \(K\) is not recovered from data.

Guardrails (well‑posedness)

\[\textbf{K1 (normalization):}\quad \int K_{\lambda}(x;X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X) < \infty.\]
\[\textbf{K2 (locality in projection):}\quad K_{\lambda}(x;X) \text{ sharply peaks for } X\approx \pi^{-1}(x).\]
\[\textbf{K3 (monotone fidelity):}\quad \alpha(\lambda) \text{ decreases with } \lambda,\ \alpha(\lambda_q)\approx 1.\]

Here \(\pi: \mathrm{MM}\to \mathbb{R}^{3,1}\) is the projection and \(\alpha\) quantifies retention.

Kernel invariants (operational)

  • Resolution \(\lambda_q\): scale where fine structure starts to retain.
  • Locality radius: how sharply MM points are tied to 4D points.
  • Regularity/positivity: well‑posed operator behavior (PSD, integrability).
  • Effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\): controls mode counting; enters lab spectra and entropy scalings.

Retention & coherence law

Fidelity to fine structure decays monotonically with probe scale:

\[\alpha(\lambda)=\exp\!\Big[-\big(\tfrac{\lambda}{\lambda_q}\big)^{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\Big].\]

This yields two regimes and a coherence cliff near \(\lambda\approx\lambda_q\): scans across the boundary should show a step‑like change in interference/coherence observables.

Phenomenology threads (tests)

S1 — ALP ladder (lab handle). A light axion‑like tower with a base around \(m_1\approx 65\,\mathrm{neV}\) (\(f_1\approx 15.717\,\mathrm{MHz}\)) and non‑harmonic spacing

\[\frac{m_n}{m_1}=n^{1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}}.\]

S2 — Coherence cliff (pm scale). Interferometric and photonic platforms should see a sharp retention transition as the effective probe crosses \(\lambda_q\).

S3 — Torsion / extra polarization (mHz band). Large‑energy hubs could yield a strain

\[h_T\sim \epsilon_T\,\frac{G\,E}{c^4 r}.\]

Each signal has a falsification gate: decisive nulls at forecasted sensitivity rule out the associated claim.

Falsification posture

  • Exclude the ALP base window and non‑harmonic ratios at forecast sensitivity → the ladder claim fails.
  • No coherence transition near the posited \(\lambda_q\) across platforms → the retention law as stated fails.
  • Null torsion/extra polarization at modeled levels → that channel fails.

The invariants remain fixed; we do not “rescue” the idea by unconstrained kernel tweaks.
