Prediction: Existence of an ALP with mass 65 neV and a non-harmonic spectrum

\[ m_n = 65 \text{ neV} \times n^{1/1.72} \]

Experimental program:

Experiment Timeline Status

Experimental program:

Experiment Institution Timeline

S3 — Fractal structure of the Universe

Prediction: Scale-dependent change in the effective fractal dimension ΔDh = 0.25 ± 0.05 near z ≈ 1.2 (DREAM layered-projection)

✔ Recent observations: Multiscale studies report scale-varying Dh; the Saraswati supercluster shows D ≈ 1.75–2 (sheet/filament morphology)

Fractal and multifractal analyses of SDSS, UltraVISTA, and other surveys (e.g., Einasto et al., 2020; Teles et al., 2021) together with the Saraswati supercluster indicate that the effective fractal dimension is a continuous function of scale—matching the DREAM expectation from layered projection.

Experimental program

Telescope / Survey Data Analysis window
DESI (Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument) Five-year spectroscopic clustering catalog 2025+
Euclid (ESA) Wide-area photometry & clustering maps 2026
LSST / Rubin Observatory Deep imaging for structure & void statistics 2026+

Method: Wavelet and (multi)fractal analysis of galaxy and void distributions for 0.5 < z < 2.0, using band-pass layers to isolate scale-dependent behavior.

Falsification criterion: If DESI/Euclid analyses find |ΔDh| < 0.1 across z < 2.0, the specific DREAM claim (ΔD ≈ 0.25) is falsified.

Refs: Saraswati (Raj et al., 2019) — ADS; Multiscale properties (Einasto et al., 2020) — A&A; Large-sample assessment (Teles et al., 2021) — arXiv.
