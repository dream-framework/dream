What this shows (everyday view)

Slide the window size \( \lambda \). When the window is small, quick details matter. Around the coherence scale \( \lambda_q \), detail drops fast — the cliff. Once the window is large, tiny, fast events cancel out. That’s why seconds can’t move months: they’re averaged inside the month-sized window.

  • Left of ridge (\(\lambda \ll \lambda_q\)): micro-events survive.
  • On ridge (\(\lambda \approx \lambda_q\)): rapid loss — hand-off from detail to coarse.
  • Right of ridge (\(\lambda \gg \lambda_q\)): only aggregates remain.

Formal frame (S2)

\[ R(\lambda)=\exp\!\left[-\left(\frac{\lambda}{\lambda_q}\right)^{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\right],\qquad y(\lambda)=\ln\!\big(-\ln R\big)=D_{\mathrm{eff}}\ln\lambda-D_{\mathrm{eff}}\ln\lambda_q. \]

The slope of \(y\) vs \(\ln\lambda\) estimates \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\); the intercept sets \(\lambda_q\).
