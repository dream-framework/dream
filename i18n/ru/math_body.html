Mathematical Frame (minimal)

Observables as push‑forwards of MM fields; guardrails for well‑posedness.

Observables as push‑forward

Let \(X\in \mathrm{MM}\), \(x\in \mathbb{R}^{3,1}\). A projection kernel \(K_{\lambda}(x;X)\) with scale \(\lambda\) defines

\[ O(x)=\int_{\mathrm{MM}} K_{\lambda}(x;X)\,\Phi(X)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{MM}}(X),\qquad \lambda=\lambda_q \text{ for our slice.} \]

Empirical content lives in the invariants: \(\lambda_q\), locality radius, regularity (positivity/PSD operator), and \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\).

Guardrails

  • Normalization: projected norms are finite.
  • Locality‑in‑projection: \(K_\lambda\) is sharply peaked near \(x\!\approx\!\pi(X)\).
  • Monotone fidelity: the retention law \(\alpha(\lambda)\) decreases with scale.
\[ \alpha(\lambda)=\exp\!\Big[-\big(\tfrac{\lambda}{\lambda_q}\big)^{D_{\mathrm{eff}}}\Big]. \]

Эффективный спектр

\(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\) packages internal‑sector mode counting and sets the non‑harmonic KK ladder.

\[ \frac{m_n}{m_1}=n^{1/D_{\mathrm{eff}}},\qquad m_1\!\approx\!65\,\mathrm{neV}. \]
