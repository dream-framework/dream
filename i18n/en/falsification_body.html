Falsification

Clear fail gates phrased in terms of kernel invariants and instrument observables.

What “falsifiable” means here

  • Claims at the level of invariants (\(\lambda_q\), \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\)).
  • No “rescuing” by unconstrained kernel tweaks.
  • Cross‑platform checks to remove instrument confounders.

Pre‑registration

  1. Set priors for \(\lambda_q\) and \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\).
  2. Specify the map from dials to effective \(\lambda\).
  3. Fix windows and rung positions.
  4. Define fail gates up front.
  5. Publish pipeline and injections.

Gate S1 — Ladder

Claim: base \(m_1\!\approx\!65\,\mathrm{neV}\) with non‑harmonic ratios.

Fail: exclusion of base & ratios at stated sensitivity.

Gate S2 — Coherence cliff

Claim: transition near \(\lambda\!\approx\!\lambda_q\) per the retention law.

Fail: no transition across repeated controlled scans.

Gate S3 — Torsion/extra polarization

Claim: detectable templates in the mHz band.

Fail: nulls at modeled levels across template families.
