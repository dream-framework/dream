Overview

What DREAM Asserts

The 4D world is a finite‑resolution projection of a 10‑dimensional Meta‑Manifold (MM) through a fixed, non‑invertible kernel. Observables are push‑forwards of MM fields; what’s empirical are the kernel’s invariants (resolution \(\lambda_q\), locality radius, regularity/positivity, effective spectral exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\)).

This page gives intuition and a map to other tabs; compact formulas live in “Math Frame”.

Intuition

A 10→4 projection with finite resolution “weaves” high‑D structure into every 4D locus. Sub‑resolution detail is averaged out; large‑scale classes and organization survive—and that’s what physics measures.

Constants \(\{c,\hbar,G,\ldots\}\) are treated as kernel‑fixed parameters in our slice (Axiom A4), not quantities to be derived.
