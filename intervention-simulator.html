Four natural experiments

The simulator above models the dynamic confirmed by these four real-world cases:

★ Sweden FTT (1984–1991): the reversal test

Sweden imposed a financial transaction tax in 1984. Equity turnover dropped ~40%. The tax was repealed in 1991. Turnover returned.

During tax: D = 0.72 (below extraction threshold — turnover decayed slowly, extraction suppressed)
After repeal: D = 4.74 (deep into extraction — turnover shot back, re-extraction accelerated)

This is the strongest evidence: a reversible prediction — intervention lowers D, removal raises it — is much harder to dismiss than a one-way correlation.

China minor gaming ban (2021)

China capped minors' gaming at 3 hours/week. Excessive gaming prevalence dropped from 28% to 4%.

Before ban: D = 4.15 (severe extraction)
After ban: D = 0.12 (natural regime)

Montréal Protocol (1987)

CFCs banned globally. Stratospheric ozone decline slowed, then stabilized.

Before ban: D = 1.27 (extraction — ozone declining super-exponentially)
After ban: D = 0.58 (natural — decline slowed to sub-exponential)

Volcker Rule (2012)

US banks banned from proprietary trading. Prop trading volume decayed over the following years.

Baseline financial D: ~1.5
Post-Volcker prop trading decay: D = 0.97 (at threshold — extraction suppressed)

Result: in all four cases, the estimated D moved downward during the intervention. In the Sweden case, D increased again after repeal.
