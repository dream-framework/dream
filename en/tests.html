How to read this registry

Each card reports the stretched-exponential exponent \(D_{\mathrm{eff}}\) from fitting \(R(\lambda)\) on a retention curve. Green values (\(D<1\)) mean the system is on the natural side of the coherence cliff; coral values (\(D>1\)) mean extraction — the channel is being drained faster than natural decay. Amber marks threshold cases (\(D\approx1\)), weak fits (where the S2 exponent hit a parameter bound) and partial confirmations.

Verdict legend: CONFIRMED — quantitative S2 win on AIC/BIC; CONSISTENT — qualitative literature match, no D extracted; NATURAL — \(D<1\); EXTRACTION — \(D>1\); PARTIAL — pipeline-compatible but awaiting data; INCONCLUSIVE — poor fit; WEAK — fit hit a parameter bound; PENDING — data not yet fit.

Intervention tests (Sweden FTT, China gaming ban, Montréal Protocol, Volcker Rule) report \(D\) before and after the policy change. A confirmed intervention is one where \(D\) crossed from extraction to natural (or back) as predicted. To add a new test, append an object to the TESTS array in this page’s source.
