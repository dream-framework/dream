Projection Instability Principle (PIP)

Under the universal retention law S2, increasing structural complexity eventually reaches a critical projection strain. Beyond this point, static projections become unstable, and stable solutions require recursive, self-maintaining dynamics. Biological life is one manifestation of this projection instability.

S2 doesn't create life. S2 creates pressure. The projection is trying to represent an increasingly complex 10D object with limited 4D bandwidth. At low complexity, a static projection is sufficient. Beyond a threshold, a static projection becomes unstable — not because information isn't lost, but because the retained information is too interconnected to be represented statically.

The Shadow Analogy

Imagine a simple sphere illuminated by the Sun. Its shadow is smooth because the underlying geometry is simple. Now imagine an intricate statue with deep cavities, overlapping surfaces, and fine ornamentation. The shadow is no longer just a silhouette — you get gradients, penumbras, multiple overlapping shadows, and fine texture.

Life is analogous to that phenomenon. The Meta-Manifold contains an extremely intricate high-dimensional structure. The 4D universe is its projection through the kernel \(K_\lambda\). As the underlying geometry becomes richer, the projection develops localized regions of higher informational density — "twirls" or folds. Life appears when this density exceeds the capacity of a static 4D representation.

Falsification

Every known living system should exhibit a measurable positive information-restoration rate that exceeds passive retention loss. Systems approaching death should show this ratio falling toward one and then below one. Abiogenesis would occur when prebiotic chemical networks first cross this threshold.

The difficult part: defining and measuring "information restored" in a physically rigorous way (algorithmic information, mutual information, or thermodynamic entropy production). Until such a measure is specified, this remains a hypothesis extending DREAM rather than a consequence of its current axioms.
